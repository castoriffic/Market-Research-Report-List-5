<p><p>The wearable antenna market is segmented into L-band, S-band, C-band, and X-band, each catering to specific communication needs. L-band operates at 1-2 GHz, ideal for GPS and mobile communications. S-band (2-4 GHz) is used for radar and satellite links. C-band (4-8 GHz) supports wireless transmissions for telecommunications and satellites. X-band (8-12 GHz) is favored in military and avionics applications due to its high precision. These bands enable diverse functionality in wearable tech, enhancing connectivity and communication.</p></p>
