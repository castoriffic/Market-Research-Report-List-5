<p><p>Ophthalmic Viscosurgical Devices (OVDs) are used in eye surgeries to maintain space, protect delicate eye tissues, and aid in the visualization of a surgeon's field. There are three main types of OVDs: Dispersive, Cohesive, and Combined. Dispersive OVDs have higher viscosity and spread over a larger area, Cohesive OVDs have a lower viscosity and form a cohesive gel, and Combined OVDs combine the properties of both Dispersive and Cohesive OVDs. Each type of OVD has specific characteristics that make them suitable for different procedures in ophthalmic surgery.</p></p>
