<p><p>The smoked fish market is primarily categorized into two types: hot smoked fish and cold smoked fish. Hot smoked fish is subjected to high temperatures during the smoking process, resulting in fully-cooked products that are flavorful and flaky, often enjoyed as a ready-to-eat option. Cold smoked fish, on the other hand, is smoked at lower temperatures, allowing for preservation while retaining a raw texture. This method imparts a delicate smoky flavor, making it ideal for gourmet applications and enhancing culinary presentations.</p></p>
