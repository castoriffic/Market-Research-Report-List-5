<p><p>High speed biophotonic sensors can be classified into two main types: Two-Dimensional Sensors and Three-Dimensional Sensors. Two-Dimensional Sensors capture images with height and width dimensions, providing valuable information about the surface characteristics of biological samples. Three-Dimensional Sensors, on the other hand, capture images with height, width, and depth dimensions, allowing for a more comprehensive analysis of biological samples and enabling researchers to gain a deeper understanding of their structures and properties. Both types of sensors play a crucial role in advancing biophotonic technologies.</p></p>
