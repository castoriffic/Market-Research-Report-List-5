<p><p>Aircraft radomes are protective coverings that house radar systems on aircraft. Nose radomes are installed on the front of the aircraft to protect the weather radar antenna. Fuselage mounted radomes are larger in size and are mounted on the fuselage to accommodate antennas for communication, navigation, or surveillance systems. Nose radomes are typically smaller and more aerodynamic, while fuselage mounted radomes are designed to house larger radar equipment for enhanced capabilities such as weather detection or communication with ground stations.</p></p>
