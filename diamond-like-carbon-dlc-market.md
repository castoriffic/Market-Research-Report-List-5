<p><p>Diamond-like Carbon (DLC) is a form of amorphous carbon that exhibits properties similar to diamonds, such as high hardness, low friction, and excellent wear resistance. The DLC market is primarily categorized into two types: Pure DLC and Mixed DLC. Pure DLC consists solely of carbon atoms and offers superior hardness and chemical stability, making it ideal for precision tools and implants. Mixed DLC, on the other hand, incorporates additional elements like hydrogen or silicon, enhancing its properties for specific applications, such as coatings in electronics and automotive industries.</p></p>
