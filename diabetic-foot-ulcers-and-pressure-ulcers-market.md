<p><p>Diabetic foot ulcers (DFUs) are severe sores or wounds that occur on the feet of individuals with diabetes, primarily due to neuropathy and peripheral arterial disease. These ulcers can lead to infections and, in severe cases, may require amputation. Pressure ulcers, also known as bedsores or decubitus ulcers, develop when sustained pressure cuts off blood flow to the skin, commonly affecting individuals with limited mobility. Both conditions pose significant health risks and economic burdens, necessitating effective treatment solutions.</p><p>The Diabetic Foot Ulcers and Pressure Ulcers Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by the rising prevalence of diabetes globally, an aging population, and an increased focus on wound care management. Innovative products, such as advanced dressings, negative pressure wound therapy, and bioengineered skin substitutes, are gaining traction. Additionally, growing awareness about the importance of diabetic foot care and preventative measures is influencing market expansion.
