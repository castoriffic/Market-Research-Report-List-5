<p><p>The microwavable foods market comprises three primary types: shelf-stable, chilled, and frozen foods. Chilled foods are perishable and require refrigeration before cooking, often offering fresher ingredients. Frozen foods are preserved at sub-zero temperatures, maintaining quality and extending shelf life until ready to cook. Each type caters to different consumer needs for convenience and meal options.</p></p>
