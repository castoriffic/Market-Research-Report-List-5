Monitoring ECG electrodes are typically used for continuous monitoring of a patient's heart activity, such as during surgery or in the ICU. Diagnostic ECG electrodes, on the other hand, are used for recording specific ECG readings for diagnostic purposes, such as detecting irregular heart rhythms or identifying signs of a heart attack. Both types of electrodes play a crucial role in monitoring and diagnosing heart conditions in medical settings.</p></p>
