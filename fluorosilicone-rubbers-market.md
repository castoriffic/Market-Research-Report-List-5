<p><p>The fluorosilicone rubber market comprises two primary types: High Temperature Vulcanization (HTV) and Room Temperature Vulcanization (RTV). HTV fluorosilicone rubbers are cured through a high-temperature process, resulting in superior heat and chemical resistance, making them ideal for demanding applications in aerospace and automotive industries. In contrast, RTV fluorosilicone rubbers cure at ambient temperatures, offering convenience and easier application for sealing and bonding, often used in consumer goods and lightweight automotive components. Both types serve distinct industrial needs.</p></p>
