<p><p>The automotive steering system market includes two primary types: Electronic Steering and Hydraulic Steering. Electronic Steering, also known as Electric Power Steering (EPS), uses an electric motor to assist steering, offering better fuel efficiency, precision, and adaptability to advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS). Hydraulic Steering relies on fluid pressure for assistance, providing a robust and responsive feel but with higher energy consumption. Both systems cater to different vehicle needs, balancing performance, cost, and technological advancements in modern automotive design.</p></p>
