<p><p>Mercury Telluride detectors are classified into two main market types: photovoltaic and photoconductive. </p><p>Photovoltaic detectors generate a voltage when exposed to infrared radiation, making them suitable for applications requiring energy conversion, such as thermal imaging. In contrast, photoconductive detectors change their electrical conductivity in response to infrared light, offering rapid response times and high sensitivity, ideal for spectroscopy and imaging applications. Together, these technologies cater to diverse industrial, scientific, and military needs in detecting infrared radiation.</p></p>
